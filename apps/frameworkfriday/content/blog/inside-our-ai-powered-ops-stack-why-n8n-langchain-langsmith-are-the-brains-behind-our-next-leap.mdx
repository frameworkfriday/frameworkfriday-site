By late 2024, we recognized that "80% of a modern operator's day can be automated."

After years scaling multi-brand eCommerce operations into the hundreds of millions, we saw the cracks in legacy tooling. So in 2025, we hit reset. We're rebuilding the entire stack around agentic AI — not shiny features, but deeply integrated workflows that reduce error, reclaim hours, and evolve in real time.

## The Core Tools We're Betting On

Each layer of our AI-powered ops stack plays a critical role:

### n8n

**What it does:** Acts as the automation brain-stem — links all apps, fires triggers, logs every edge-case.

**Why we chose it:** Open-source flexibility. We can ship community-ready JSON workflows in minutes.

### LangChain + LangSmith

**What they do:** Manage decision flows, memory, and evaluations when logic gets fuzzy.

**Why we chose them:** Keeps humans in the judgment seat while agents handle the repetition.

### LangGraph (Watching Closely)

**What it could do:** Enable graph-based orchestration for advanced multi-agent handoffs.

**Why it's on our radar:** Not yet in production, but the roadmap is promising.

## Use Case Snapshot: Order-Tracking Agent

One of our first pilots is the Order-Tracking GPT Agent, built using n8n and GPT. Here's how it works:

**1. Vendor Emails Arrive**

Vendor partners email tracking numbers. These messages forward to a dedicated inbox where the n8n flow starts.

**2. GPT Parses Tracking Data**

A GPT node reads the email, extracts tracking details, and tags the corresponding order ID.

**3. Zoho CRM Update**

The parsed data is injected into the customer record in Zoho CRM via an n8n node.

**4. Customer Notification**

CRM automation sends an email update with tracking info to the customer — automatically.

## Why It Matters

- **Error resistance:** Agents follow consistent logic — no more typos or skipped updates.
- **Near real-time delivery:** Customers get updates seconds after vendors send tracking.
- **Team redeployment:** What once took 5 hours/day now happens on autopilot, freeing ops analysts to focus on exceptions and edge cases.

## Early Wins We're Seeing

- Tasks run flawlessly, every time — significantly reduced error rates
- Roles are evolving — operators are becoming system owners, not button-clickers
- Savings are real — costs are dropping without layoffs, and time is reinvested into R&D and upskilling
